Pyramids of Giza

4.8/5

The Pyramids of Giza, built over 4,500 years ago, are the last surviving wonder of the ancient world. The Great Pyramid, constructed for Pharaoh Khufu, was the tallest man-made structure for over 3,800 years at 146.5 meters. This complex includes the pyramids of Khufu, Khafre, and Menkaure, surrounded by smaller pyramids and cemeteries.

  • Giza Plateau, Giza
  • 5M+ Visitors
  • Built: 2630 BC

4.6/5

Abu Simbel Temples

4.9/5

The Abu Simbel Temples, built by Ramses II in the 13th century BC, are renowned for their massive rock-cut statues. The Great Temple features four colossal statues of Ramses II, each 20 meters tall, and was relocated in the 1960s to save it from flooding.

  • Abu Simbel, Aswan
  • 1M+ Visitors
  • Built: 1264 BC

Saladin Citadel

4.5/5

The Saladin Citadel, built in the 12th century by Salah al-Din (Saladin), overlooks Cairo and houses the stunning Mohamed Ali Mosque. It served as the seat of government for nearly 700 years and offers panoramic views of the city.

  • Cairo, Egypt
  • 3M+ Visitors
  • Built: 1176 AD
